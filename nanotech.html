Nanotechnology

Nanotechnology is the science behind to deliberately make materials at the nanoscale to take advantage of their enhanced properties such as higher strength, lighter weight, increased control of light spectrum, and greater chemical reactivity than their larger-scale counterparts. Imagine raw material to have the particles sizes varying from micronmeter to millimeters as compared to nanomaterial which has a particle size of 1 to 100 nanometers in diameter, exhibiting unusual physical and electronic properties. Technically speaking, particle size less than 100 nanometers is considered true nano.

By using nano-scale polymers, it penetrates into unparalleled surface which bonds extremely well and addding coating density to greatly improves resistance to environmental threats and harsh elements.

Manufacturing at nano scale differs from molecular manufacturing which requires controlling the precision of the assembly of nanostructures. To obtain that highest precision, testing for defect controls takes up more time as it requires more selective and careful handling, This resulted in higher capital expenditure in the equipments and higher manufacturing cost.

In 2004, CarLack further improved the formula by introducing their nano technology (each molecule = 1,000,000/mm3 = one millionth of a cubed millimetre) formula application into Carlack Systematic Care. In layman terms, that means better adhesion to the paintwork and to itself giving a greater density of acrylic seal. While there are similar products that uses acrylic-based as sealant, the applied nanotechnology clearly differentiate it.
