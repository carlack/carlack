    Inherent properties within the polymer

Polyethylene

First synthesized in Germany in 1898, it has revolutionalised our life in many aspects. Properties would be of low strength, hardness and rigidity, but has a high ductility and impact strength. For commercial grade high density polyethylene, the melting point is up to 160 degree celcius. This melting point, in our tropical weather, is critical to withstand the harsh weather especially above the bonnet area.

By using nano-scale polymers, it penetrates into unparalleled surface which bonds extremely well and adding coating density to greatly improves resistance to environmental threats and harsh elements.

Acrylic

Developed in Germany as early as 1934, Acrylic has commercialised in the 1950s for paint. Acrylic is water soluble but becomes water resistant when it is dry. due to its hardness, it can potentially harden the surface by 30% to increse the riginess. Noted for their transparency, it allow more light to penetrate into the coating and makes the metallic flake in the paint shine through. Under close observation, you can compare the layer of coat applied by Carlack in comparison with an optical grade glass to produce the unobstructed clarity.
